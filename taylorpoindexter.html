About

Taylor Poindexter is the co-founder of Black Code Collective , an organization that strives to provide a safe space for Black Engineers to collaborate and grow their skills, while also increasing civic engagement. In addition to this, she is a Senior Back End Engineer for a startup called tech co. - a company that is working to make a more participatory democracy. When asked about what book Taylor would recommend to engineers everywhere, Taylor suggested The Pragmatic Programmer. She said, "It's a very popular book, and for good reason. It will change the way you attack problems and make you a more efficient programmer overall."

On the day-to-day

"On the back end, I'm handling all the data that is passed to the front end for you to be able to see and manipulate. I also write the algorithms to potentially be able to save that data to the database and then serve to the user when they come back."

On the challenges

"The amount of imposter syndrome I've felt until the last 6 months has been at times overwhelming and there were genuine times when I thought that I couldn't make it in tech.""

On mentorship

"I have several mentors I've picked up along the way but they've all happened very naturally. And I think those are the best types of mentor relationships because they genuinely care about the advancement of my career."
